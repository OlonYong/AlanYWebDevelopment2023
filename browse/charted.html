Uncharted

Image Source: Plex

PG-13

2022

My review

Much better than I expected, I was cautiously optimistic by the trailer and had hope Tom Holland could play a young Nathan Drake, and hell yeah, he can! He was amazing in the role, fully embraced it a younger version of the character. Love how he is introduced as a street-smart guy who has a cool bad-boy side to him as he flirts with customers to get close to them before he picks pockets them, very entertaining to see. Did not get any Peter Parker vibes at all.

Very funny, action is entertaining, though but is very reliant on CGI (which for the most part is solid). Plot is simple but done well and very fast-paced. Also well cast, Tom Holland is amazing as Nate, Mark Wahlberg while not as believable as Sully, is still charming and does have some moments that make him a fine younger Sully. Sophia Ali is great as Chloe, and Tati Gabrielle makes a threatening villain. Full of surprises, twists, and betrayals. The tone while light, does have some darker, more intense moments which really works.

It is not 100% faithful the games and kind of falls apart when you realise it is a prequel, but it works better if you think of the film on a different timeline then the games. Intriguing cliff-hanger at the post-credits scene, hope this is financially successful and they make a sequel. 8/10 as a film, 5-6/10 as an Uncharted film.
