Elvis

Image Source: Warner Bros

15+

2022

My review

The man. The legend. The King of rock & roll. First two acts were a bit more lascivious than I thought it’d be, he really got a fanbase and criticism from simply his wiggle dance? Different times I guess. If you are a fan of Elvis I think you’ll love this. It’s not only Elvis’ story but the colonel’s too. Parker was really leeching Elvis, he helped turn him into the biggest star the world had ever seen but to his own benefit. A good chunk of the movie is told from his perspective as he narrates his side of it. I was enjoying the movie but at some point it started to feel too long, still good in the end. Austin butler really carries the film, not an easy task to outshine Tom Hanks but he did, it’ll probably do wonders for his career. A little surprised they didn’t use cosmetics to age Austin a little more, he looked the same age throughout but it’s a small gremlin.

Third act of the movie was a more personal dive into Elvis’s struggles, became less about the music and more about Elvis himself and thought that was great. I think the whole movie in general was a good way to honour his legacy and Austin butler’s impersonation of him was spot on. I was surprised at how they managed to squeeze his whole career into just one sitting because his life was pretty hectic. Biography films may not be everyone’s taste, and this may not be the best biopic out there, but it barely puts a foot wrong, so I think it’s worth a watch.

It also gave me insight into the whole “Elvis appropriated black culture” issue, and maybe I don’t feel as strongly about that because it’s really black American culture and not mine per se (African) but looking at his upbringing I don’t think it was as malicious as it’s made up to be, seems like it was just the music he grew up around. He was a result of his environment if this movie was anything to go by.
