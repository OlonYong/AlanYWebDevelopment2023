Inception

Image Source: Amazon

15+

2010

My review

Watching this movie was like going on a mind-blowing mind bender, right from the start. I love the opening scene and how it kind of starts off like real dreams do, in that you just find yourself in a dream -- there's no definitive beginning -- you just kinda of "wash up on shore" of the dream sort to speak. I thought that was very symbolic and full circle for how the movie ends when you are left to question what's real and what's a part of Cobb's dream as we watch his totem spin. Or is it all a dream? This film is an event within itself, and anybody who doesn't understand it or get it definitely doesn't know great filmmaking.

This movie was very different than any other movie that I can think of to compare it to. And it certainly is legendary. Needless to say, I love Leo. The roles he chooses always capitalize on his talents perfectly. Some of the best acting of our time. He is captivating to watch from start to finish and always delivers the right emotional depth for the character and situation. Just on point expertise with ease.

The overarching concept of implanting a thought or idea into someone's mind and having things transgress from there was very intriguing. The power of this concept really stuck with me and manifested in the film very effectively. Extremely smart film that doesn't bother to dumb itself down. There were so many different, interwoven little plot titbits that sprung out in different directions, but all were vital to the foundation of the storyline. Well written.

I liked the exploration of dreams and the different levels of dreams and questioning and bending of reality. All of that was in my realm so this movie was very intriguing to me from beginning to end. No spoilers.
